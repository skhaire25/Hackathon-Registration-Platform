What is HackForge?

HackForge is a 48-hour build sprint for students and early-career engineers — a proving ground where an idea on a whiteboard becomes a working demo by Sunday evening. No lectures, no filler: just a problem statement, a team, and a deadline.

Since its first edition, HackForge has pulled together participants from over 120 colleges to ship projects across AI, fintech, healthtech, and open innovation — judged by engineers who've actually shipped production code.

Frequently asked questions

Still stuck after reading these? Reach out to the team.

Who can participate?
Any currently enrolled undergraduate or postgraduate student is eligible. You'll need a valid college ID at check-in.
What's the maximum team size?
Teams can have up to 4 members. Solo participants can register individually and get matched during team formation.
What are the eligibility requirements?
Open to students across all years and departments. At least one team member must have basic familiarity with version control (Git).
Will I get a certificate?
Yes — every registered participant who checks in and submits a project receives a digital certificate, downloadable from the dashboard after the event.
What do I need to submit?
A GitHub repository link, a short presentation deck, and a demo video are required. A live deployed demo is optional but strongly recommended.
How is judging decided?
Projects are scored across four criteria: innovation, technical execution, presentation quality, and bonus points for real-world impact.
